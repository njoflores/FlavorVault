Pancit Malabon

Pancit Malabon is a flavorful noodle dish from Malabon City, known for its seafood toppings and thick rice noodles. It’s a perfect meal for special occasions and gatherings, often featuring shrimp, smoked fish flakes, squid, and a variety of vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb thick rice noodles
  • 1/2 lb pork belly, boiled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup annatto seeds, diluted in 1/2 cup water (annatto water)
  • 3/4 cup shrimp juice
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp fresh garlic, minced
  • 1 medium-sized onion, minced
  • 1/4 cup pork rinds (chicharon), pounded
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, shelled, cooked, and halved lengthwise
  • 1/4 lb adobong pusit (squid), sliced
  • 1/2 cup napa cabbage (pechay Baguio), chopped and blanched
  • 1 piece lemon, quartered
  • 3/4 cup tinapa flakes (smoked fish flakes)
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1 tbsp parsley, chopped
  • 2 tbsp toasted garlic

Instructions:

  1. Step 1:
    Cook the noodles according to package instructions. If using traditional Pancit Malabon noodles, soak them overnight before cooking. Set aside.
  2. Step 2:
    In a pan, sauté minced garlic and onion until fragrant. Add chopped boiled pork and cook for 3 minutes.
  3. Step 3:
    Add fish sauce and ground black pepper, then stir. Pour in shrimp juice and annatto water, bringing it to a boil.
  4. Step 4:
    Stir in pounded pork rinds (chicharon) and simmer for 2-3 minutes before turning off the heat.
  5. Step 5:
    Place the cooked noodles in a large bowl. Pour in the sauce mixture and mix well.
  6. Step 6:
    Transfer the noodles and sauce to a wide serving plate. Arrange tinapa flakes, eggs, parsley, garlic, and cabbage on top.
  7. Step 7:
    Serve with lemon or calamansi on the side and enjoy!
