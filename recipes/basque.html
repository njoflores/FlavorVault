Basque Cheesecake

A light, mousse-like cheesecake with a caramelized "burnt" surface. This iconic dessert from Spain's Basque Country combines creamy vanilla flavors with a touch of caramel for a dessert that's as beautiful as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 500g cream cheese (Philadelphia recommended)
  • 220g whisked eggs (approx. 4–5 large eggs)
  • 250ml whipping cream
  • 150g caster sugar
  • 10g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract or bean paste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F) or 200°C (fan-forced). Line a 6cm-deep cake pan with scrunched baking paper for the classic rustic look.
  2. Beat the cream cheese until smooth using a hand mixer. Add sugar and beat until just combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the flour with a small amount of cream to form a lump-free paste. Gradually add the rest of the cream and vanilla, whisking until smooth.
  4. Slowly add the cream mixture to the cream cheese while beating on low speed. Stop mixing as soon as combined.
  5. Gradually add the whisked eggs while beating on low speed. Stop as soon as incorporated to avoid bubbles.
  6. Pour the batter into the lined pan. Tap the pan on the counter to release air bubbles, then pop visible bubbles with a knife.
  7. Bake for 45–65 minutes until the surface is deeply golden brown and the center jiggles like jelly. Adjust the time based on your oven.
  8. Cool at room temperature for 2 hours, then refrigerate uncovered for at least 8 hours or overnight.
  9. Slice and serve. No toppings needed – the cheesecake shines on its own!

Tips:

  • Use full-fat cream cheese and whipping cream for the best texture and flavor.
  • Measure your eggs carefully – the balance is key to achieving the perfect consistency.
  • Refrigerate uncovered to avoid condensation and preserve the cheesecake's shiny surface.
