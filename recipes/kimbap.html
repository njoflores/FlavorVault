Kimbap

Kimbap is a Korean-style sushi roll with a variety of cooked vegetables and meats, wrapped in roasted Nori seaweed. This flavorful dish is popular in Japan and can be made with a mix of fillings such as seasoned beef, cooked vegetables, and Tamagoyaki (rolled egg). It’s a fun and delicious challenge to make, and the effort is definitely worth it!

Ingredients:

  • 2 inches Takuan, pickled yellow radish
  • 2 sheets Nori roasted seaweed (8" square)
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tsp sesame seeds
  • Seasoned Rice:
    • 2 cups steamed rice
    • 1/4 tsp salt
    • 1/2 Tbsp sesame oil
  • Cooked Burdock:
    • 9 inches burdock
    • 1 tsp sesame oil
    • 1 Tbsp sugar
    • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
    • 1/2 Tbsp mirin
    • 2 Tbsp water
  • Cooked Carrot:
    • 1 small carrot
    • 1/2 Tbsp sesame oil
    • 1/8 tsp salt
    • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • Cooked Spinach:
    • 1/2 bunch spinach
    • 1 tsp soy sauce
    • 1 tsp sugar
    • 1/8 tsp salt
    • 1 tsp sesame oil
    • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • Tamagoyaki Rolled Egg:
    • 3 eggs
    • 1/8 tsp salt
    • 1 tsp soy sauce
    • 1 tsp mirin
    • 2 tsp oil

Instructions:

  1. Cut Takuan pickled yellow radish into thin matchsticks. Set aside.
  2. Make seasoned rice by mixing steamed rice with salt and sesame oil. Cover and set aside.
  3. For the cooked burdock, cut it into thin strips and stir-fry in sesame oil for 2-3 minutes. Add sugar, soy sauce, mirin, and water. Cook until the liquid is almost gone. Set aside.
  4. For the cooked carrot, peel and cut it into matchsticks. Cook in sesame oil, then season with salt and sesame seeds. Set aside.
  5. For the cooked spinach, blanch it, squeeze out excess water, and mix with soy sauce, sugar, salt, sesame oil, and sesame seeds. Set aside.
  6. For the Tamagoyaki, beat eggs with salt, soy sauce, and mirin. Cook thin layers of egg in a rectangular pan, rolling each layer as it sets. When cooled, slice into long strips.
  7. To assemble, place a sheet of Nori on a sushi mat and spread 1 cup of seasoned rice on it, leaving a small edge. Place Takuan, burdock, carrot, spinach, and Tamagoyaki horizontally in the center. Roll tightly using the mat, then slice the roll into 3/4-inch thick pieces.
  8. Brush the rolls with sesame oil and sprinkle with sesame seeds before serving.

Tips:

  • Make sure to season each filling separately to bring out the flavors.
  • If you're short on time, some fillings like cucumber can be used raw to speed up the process.
  • Prepare the fillings the day before to make assembling the Kimbap faster.
