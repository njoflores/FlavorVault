Batangas Lomi

Batangas Lomi is a thick, hearty noodle soup inspired by Chinese flavors, featuring pork, liver, and kikiam. It's topped with a variety of delicious condiments, including chicharon, meatballs, and fried garlic, giving it a unique texture and taste.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 medium white onion, sliced
  • 250g pork belly (liempo), sliced
  • 1 cup kikiam, sliced
  • 150g pork liver, sliced into strips
  • 2 pieces pork bouillon cubes
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 1/2 tbsp soy sauce (more to serve)
  • 500g fresh lomi noodles, rinsed
  • 6 tbsp cassava starch, dissolved in 3 tbsp cold water
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • Salt to taste
  • Ground black pepper to taste
  • Chicharon (crushed), for topping
  • Meatballs, for topping
  • Fried garlic, for topping
  • Chopped green onions, for topping
  • Calamansi, to serve
  • Siling labuyo (bird's eye chili), to serve
  • Chopped white onions, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Step 1:
    Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté onions until fragrant.
  2. Step 2:
    Add pork and cook until browned. Add kikiam and sauté until lightly browned. Add liver and cook until dark brown. Set aside some liver for topping.
  3. Step 3:
    Add pork bouillon cubes and mix. Then, add water and soy sauce. Cook until simmering.
  4. Step 4:
    Add the lomi noodles and simmer for about 5 minutes, or until tender.
  5. Step 5:
    Stir in dissolved cassava starch and simmer until the sauce thickens and becomes sticky, about 5 minutes.
  6. Step 6:
    Add the beaten eggs and stir immediately. Simmer for 1 minute. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Step 7:
    Divide the lomi into bowls. Add desired toppings such as chicharon, meatballs, fried garlic, and green onions. Serve with soy sauce, calamansi, red chili, and onions on the side.
