Beer-Braised Spiced Pork Shanks

This rustic yet elegant dish from Brittany features pork shanks slowly braised in amber beer and spices until fall-off-the-bone tender. Perfect for a cozy winter meal or a romantic dinner, it combines the best flavors of the region: pork, shallots, and amber beer.

Ingredients:

  • 2 whole fresh pork shanks, bone-in and skin on
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/3 lb shallots, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 lb carrots, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 8 cloves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 sprigs of thyme
  • 3 cups amber beer
  • 1 cup beef stock (low sodium)

Instructions:

  1. Step 1:
    Take the pork shanks out of the fridge an hour before cooking. Season with salt. Preheat the oven to 320°F (160°C).
  2. Step 2:
    Heat olive oil and butter in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the pork shanks for about 10 minutes, turning every 2-3 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  3. Step 3:
    In the same pot, add shallots, garlic, and carrots. Cook for 7-8 minutes. Add the pork shanks back, along with cloves, bay leaves, cinnamon, black pepper, and thyme. Pour in beer and stock, bring to a simmer.
  4. Step 4:
    Cover and transfer the pot to the oven. Braise for 3 hours, turning the shanks halfway through. If needed, cook an additional 30 minutes until tender.
  5. Step 5:
    Serve the pork shanks with the braising liquid and vegetables, paired with mashed potatoes or roasted potatoes.
