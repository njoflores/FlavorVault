Lumpia (Lumpiang Shanghai)

Lumpia, or Lumpiang Shanghai, is a Filipino-style egg roll filled with a savory mixture of ground pork, vegetables, and seasonings. Deep-fried to golden perfection, it’s a crispy, delicious snack or appetizer that’s loved by many. This version keeps the ingredients simple but flavorful, perfect for any occasion. Serve with sweet and sour sauce or banana ketchup for extra taste!

Ingredients:

  • 50 pieces lumpia wrapper
  • 3 cups cooking oil
  • Filling ingredients:
    • 1 1/2 lbs ground pork
    • 2 pieces onions, minced
    • 2 pieces carrots, minced
    • 1 1/2 teaspoons garlic powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
    • 1/2 cup parsley, chopped
    • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
    • 2 eggs

Instructions:

  1. Combine all the filling ingredients in a bowl and mix well.
  2. Scoop 1 to 1 1/2 tablespoons of filling and place it in the center of a lumpia wrapper. Spread the filling, then fold both sides of the wrapper. Fold the bottom, brush the top edge with beaten egg, and roll up tightly.
  3. Heat oil in a cooking pot. Deep fry lumpia in medium heat until it floats and turns golden brown.
  4. Remove from the pot, let excess oil drip, and serve.

Tips:

  • If you want a healthier version, consider air frying the lumpia at 370°F for 12 minutes. Lightly spray with oil for a crispier finish.
  • Adjust the seasoning to your taste. You can add garlic or other herbs to enhance the flavor.
  • For the best texture, make sure the oil is not too hot, as this can burn the wrapper before the filling is cooked through.
