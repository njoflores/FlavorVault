Spam and Radish Kimchi Fried Rice

This savory fried rice combines the bold flavors of Spam, the tang of radish kimchi (kkakdugi), and the umami kick from gochujang. Perfectly crispy and loaded with flavor, this is a comforting and satisfying dish!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (short grain rice, such as sushi rice)
  • 1 cup radish kimchi (kkakdugi), cut into small cubes
  • 100g Spam, diced into small cubes
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil (rice bran or canola oil)
  • 20g green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce (Kikkoman or Korean yangjo ganjang)
  • 2 tbsp kimchi juice (preferably from radish kimchi)
  • 1 tbsp sugar (raw sugar or brown sugar)
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 eggs (sunny-side up, optional)
  • Sesame seeds and seaweed strips for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare your ingredients: Chop the radish kimchi and dice the Spam. If using freshly cooked rice, spread it on a plate to cool and dry for 10 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add green onions and stir for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add Spam and cook until crispy and golden brown.
  3. Add the chopped kimchi and sauté for 2-3 minutes until it releases its flavors.
  4. Pour in the soy sauce, kimchi juice, sugar, gochujang, and water. Stir well to combine.
  5. Add the cooked rice, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry and let it sizzle until the rice becomes slightly crispy on the bottom.
  6. Drizzle sesame oil over the rice and stir to combine. Serve in a bowl, topping with a sunny-side-up egg, sesame seeds, and seaweed strips. Enjoy!

Tips:

  • For the best texture, use day-old rice or spread fresh rice on a tray to cool down.
  • Radish kimchi (kkakdugi) adds a crunchy texture that pairs beautifully with the Spam. If you can't find it, feel free to substitute with regular kimchi.
  • Customize with proteins like bacon, canned tuna, or even cubed steak for a more upscale version!
  • For extra crispiness, let the rice sizzle a bit longer before serving.
