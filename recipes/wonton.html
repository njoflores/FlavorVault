Beef Wonton Mami

Beef Wonton Mami is a comforting and hearty noodle soup that features dumplings, tender beef, fresh egg noodles, and a flavorful broth. Perfect for a cozy meal on a chilly day!

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 onion, peeled and sliced thinly
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 pound beef brisket, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 pieces star anise
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 beef bouillon cube or 1 tablespoon beef base
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 package (16 ounces) Mami (fresh egg noodles)
  • 1 small napa cabbage, shredded
  • Fried garlic bits
  • ½ cup green onions, chopped

For the Wontons:

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • ¼ cup green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon pepper
  • 15 pieces wonton wrappers

Instructions:

  1. In a pot over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions and garlic and cook until aromatic. Add beef and cook until lightly browned.
  2. Add water and bring to a boil, skimming scum that floats on top. When it clears, add star anise. Add brown sugar and beef bouillon. Stir until dissolved.
  3. Lower heat, cover, and cook for about 1 to 1 ½ hours or until beef is fork-tender.
  4. In the meantime, make the wontons. In a bowl, combine ground pork, green onions, garlic, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Place about 1 teaspoon of the mixture in the center of each wonton wrapper. Wet the edges with water, fold, and press to seal.
  5. Bring a pot of water to a boil and drop in the wontons. Cook for 3-4 minutes or until they float. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  6. Once the beef is cooked, add the Mami noodles to the soup and cook according to the package directions.
  7. Once the noodles are cooked, add the shredded cabbage and cooked wontons. Simmer for another 2-3 minutes.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve in bowls topped with fried garlic and chopped green onions.

Pro Tips:

  • For extra flavor, you can add a dash of soy sauce or fish sauce to the broth.
  • Make sure to cook the beef slowly to ensure it becomes tender and flavorful.

Serving Suggestions and Storage:

  • Serve hot, garnished with extra fried garlic bits and green onions.
  • The broth can be refrigerated for up to 3 days. To store, remove the beef and wontons, then reheat the broth separately before serving.
