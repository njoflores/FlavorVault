Filipino Scrambled Eggs

Filipino Scrambled Eggs is a classic breakfast dish with tomatoes and onions. It's quick and easy to make yet so hearty and tasty. Perfect for breakfast, brunch, or any meal of the day!

Ingredients:

  • 6 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 large Roma tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 large onion, peeled and sliced thinly
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, break the eggs and whisk until fluffy and without streaks of white. Set aside.
  2. In a pan over low heat, add butter. When it begins to melt, add onions and cook until softened.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook, mashing with the back of the spoon, until softened and release juice. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Add beaten eggs over the tomato-onion mixture and allow to cook without stirring for about 20 to 30 seconds.
  5. When it begins to set, gently fold with tomatoes and onions until combined.
  6. Continue to cook until eggs are just set but still slightly wet.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from the pan and serve immediately.

Pro Tips:

  • Whisk the eggs very well to aerate and create lighter, fluffier curds.
  • Cook the eggs at low heat to avoid them becoming hard and dry.
  • Turn off the heat while the eggs are still slightly wet, as they will continue to cook with the residual heat from the pan.

Serving Suggestions and Storage:

  • Serve with steamed rice, pandesal, or your favorite toast for a hearty meal.
  • Best enjoyed immediately after cooking for maximum fluffiness and flavor.
