French-style Deviled Eggs (Oeufs Mimosa)

A rich and creamy French twist on traditional deviled eggs. These "Oeufs Mimosa" feature fluffy, Dijon mustard-infused yolk filling, fresh herbs, and a delicate touch of paprika for extra flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup (60g) mayonnaise
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 small shallot, peeled and minced
  • 1 tsp vinegar (white, champagne, or white wine)
  • 1 tbsp chives, minced (+ extra for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp flat-leaf parsley, minced
  • 1/8 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp paprika (hot, sweet, or smoked), for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Make the hard-boiled eggs:
    Place the eggs in a pot and cover with water, ensuring they’re submerged by at least 1/2 inch. Bring to a firm boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, turn off the heat, cover the pot, and set a timer for 12 minutes. Once done, transfer the eggs to a bowl of cold water and let them cool. After cooling, peel the eggs and carefully cut them in half lengthwise, separating the yolks and whites. Set the whites aside.
  2. Make the filling:
    In a mixing bowl, combine mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, minced shallot, vinegar, chives, parsley, pepper, and salt. Pass the yolks through a fine-mesh strainer to create a fluffy texture, using the back of a spoon to press them through. Reserve about 2 tablespoons of the fluffy yolks for garnish. Mix the remaining yolks with the mayonnaise mixture and adjust seasoning to taste.
  3. Assemble the eggs:
    Spoon or pipe the creamy filling into the hollows of the egg whites. Sprinkle with the reserved fluffy yolks, additional chives, and a dash of paprika for garnish.
