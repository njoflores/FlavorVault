Kimchijeon (Kimchi Pancake)

Crispy, flavor-packed kimchi pancakes made from fermented kimchi and simple pantry ingredients. Perfect as a snack, appetizer, or light meal!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup thinly sliced fully-fermented kimchi
  • 1 to 2 scallions (2 if small)
  • 1/4 medium onion
  • 3 oz minced pork, canned tuna (drained), or squid (optional)
  • Cooking oil (high smoke point, for pan-frying)

Batter:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour (or buchimgaru, Korean pancake mix)
  • 2 to 3 tbsp kimchi juice (or water)
  • 1 to 2 tsp gochujang (optional)
  • 1 lightly beaten egg (optional)
  • 1 cup icy cold water (more if not using kimchi juice)

Optional Dipping Sauce:

  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • Pinch of black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Thinly slice the kimchi, cut the scallions into 2-inch pieces, and slice the onion.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the flour, kimchi liquid (or water), gochujang, egg (optional), and water. Stir gently until well combined, ensuring the batter is thin enough to flow easily from a spoon.
  3. Stir in the kimchi, scallions, onion, and protein (if using).
  4. Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat. Spoon the batter into the pan, spreading it thinly. Cook for about 3 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
  5. Flip the pancake and add more oil to the edges of the pan. Press gently with a spatula and cook until crispy, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Repeat with remaining batter. Serve hot with dipping sauce, if desired.

Tips:

  • Use well-fermented kimchi for the best flavor.
  • Make the batter thin for a crispier texture.
  • Use icy cold water to ensure crispy pancakes.
  • Spread the batter thin in the pan for better crisping.
  • Use generous oil to get the pancakes golden and crispy.
