Tagliatelle alla Bolognese

An Italian classic: beef and vegetables simmered with wine, broth, pancetta, and tomato sauce for 3 hours. This Bolognese sauce perfectly clings to fresh tagliatelle, making it a comforting and rich dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 LB. of fresh egg tagliatelle
  • 3/4 LB. of ground beef
  • 1/3 LB. of pancetta
  • 1 1/4 CUPS of tomato passata or tomato purée
  • 3/4 CUP of beef broth
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 celery stalks
  • 1/2 small onion
  • 1/2 CUP of red (or dry white) wine
  • 1/2 CUP of milk
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Chop the pancetta and finely mince the celery, carrots, and onion.
  2. Heat a pan with a drizzle of olive oil. Sauté the pancetta, then add the vegetables and sweat until soft.
  3. Add the ground beef and sauté for 5 minutes.
  4. Add 1/2 cup of wine and allow it to completely evaporate.
  5. Add the tomato passata, stir for a few minutes, then add the milk and cover with beef broth.
  6. Simmer the sauce for 2-3 hours, adding more broth as needed and adjusting the seasoning with salt and pepper.
  7. Cook the tagliatelle in boiling salted water for 3-4 minutes or until al dente. Drain the pasta.
  8. Serve the tagliatelle with a generous amount of the Bolognese sauce.

Tips:

  • For an authentic experience, serve the Bolognese sauce with freshly made egg tagliatelle.
  • Feel free to adjust the seasoning and broth levels during the long simmer to suit your taste.
