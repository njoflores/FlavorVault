Ricotta Cheese Tortelloni with Butter and Sage

A classic butter and sage sauce pairs perfectly with ricotta and parsley stuffed tortelloni. Simple yet delicious, this dish is perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 LB 6 OZ of all-purpose flour
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 LB. of ricotta cheese
  • Chopped parsley
  • Parmigiano cheese, grated
  • Nutmeg, to taste
  • Unsalted butter
  • Sage leaves

Instructions:

  1. Combine the flour and eggs. Knead until the dough is compact and smooth (around 2 lbs.). Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest overnight.
  2. For the filling: Chop a handful of parsley and mix it with ricotta, 1 Tbsp. of a beaten egg, salt, 1 Tbsp. of grated Parmigiano, and plenty of nutmeg.
  3. Roll out the dough until it's about 1/16" thick. Cut into squares. Spoon an abundant tablespoon of filling into the center of each square. Fold over and press to seal, forming the tortelloni.
  4. For the sauce, melt the butter in a small saucepan (1 1/2 Tbsp. butter per person) with sage leaves. Pour over the tortelloni and serve with freshly grated Parmigiano cheese.

Tips:

  • Resting the dough overnight helps to improve its texture and makes it easier to work with.
  • For an even richer sauce, add a dash of cream to the butter and sage mixture.
