Japchae (Korean Glass Noodle Stir Fry)

Japchae is a delicious Korean dish made with stir-fried sweet potato starch noodles, vegetables, and beef. It's known for its rich flavor, savory-sweet sauce, and satisfying texture. Perfect for any occasion!

Ingredients:

  • 250g Korean sweet potato starch noodles (dangmyeon, 당면)
  • 100g rib eye fillet, cut into strips
  • 1 carrot, peeled & julienned
  • 110g baby spinach
  • 1/4 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 1/2 yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • 100g fresh shiitake mushrooms, cleaned & sliced

Spinach Seasoning:

  • 1/4 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil

Beef Marinade:

  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp rice wine (mirin)
  • 1/2 tsp minced garlic
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil

Noodles & Mushroom Marinade:

  • 4 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 1 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 Tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1/8 tsp ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Soak the noodles in warm water for 30–40 minutes until soft, or follow package instructions for cooking.
  2. Meanwhile, combine the beef marinade ingredients and let the beef marinate for 15 minutes.
  3. Heat a pan over medium-high heat and sauté the spinach with the spinach seasoning. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same pan, sauté the beef until browned. Set aside.
  5. In the same pan, stir-fry the carrots, onions, and mushrooms for 3–5 minutes until tender.
  6. In a separate pan, heat the marinade for the noodles and mushrooms over medium heat. Add the noodles and mushrooms, and stir-fry for 2–3 minutes until coated.
  7. Combine all ingredients in a large bowl, mixing gently. Serve warm and enjoy!

Tips:

  • For a vegetarian version, replace beef with tofu or extra vegetables.
  • Feel free to add more vegetables like bell peppers or zucchini for extra flavor and texture.
  • Leftovers can be stored in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in a pan to restore the texture of the noodles.
