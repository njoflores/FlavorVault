French Cheese Puffs (Gougères)

Cheesy, crisp, and airy, Gougères are impossible to stop at just one! These bite-sized savory puffs are made from choux pastry and Gruyère cheese, perfect for appetizers or the holiday table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup (250mL) water
  • 1/3 cup (80g) unsalted butter, cut into small cubes
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg (freshly grated)
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 ¼ cups (155g) all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • ¾ cup (75g) grated Gruyère cheese (or aged cheddar)

Instructions:

  1. Step 1:
    In a medium saucepan, combine the water, cubed butter, salt, nutmeg, and ground pepper. Cover and bring to a boil. Simmer for 2 minutes. Remove from heat and immediately add in the flour. Stir vigorously with a wooden spoon until a smooth dough forms. Return the saucepan to low heat and keep stirring until the dough ‘dries out’ and pulls away from the pan, about 2 minutes.
  2. Step 2:
    Remove from the heat again and let cool for 1 minute. Beat the eggs thoroughly into the dough, one at a time. It is important that each egg is fully incorporated into the batter before adding the next egg. If the dough separates, keep beating and it will come together again.
  3. Step 3:
    Using a piping bag or a cookie scoop (or two spoons), drop tablespoon-sized balls of dough on the baking sheets, keeping them one inch apart. Sprinkle about half a tablespoon of grated Gruyère on top of each ball.
  4. Step 4:
    Bake for 30-35 minutes at 390°F (200°C), until puffed and golden. Enjoy warm!
