How to Make Espresso

Learn how to make the perfect espresso shot at home with or without an espresso machine. Rich, concentrated, and with a smooth crema, this espresso is perfect on its own or as the base for other coffee drinks.

Ingredients:

  • 9 grams espresso roast coffee for a single shot, 18 grams for a double shot
  • Espresso machine (or alternative methods listed below)
  • Knock box (optional, for tamping and discarding used grounds)

Instructions:

  1. Grind the coffee: Use espresso roast coffee and grind it very finely. Adjust the grind for your specific machine if necessary.
  2. Pack and tamp the coffee grounds: Add the coffee grounds into the espresso basket (portafilter), slightly heaping. Use a tamper to press them evenly and firmly.
  3. Pull the shot: Place the portafilter into the espresso machine and start the shot. A well-pulled shot takes 25-30 seconds and results in a rich, creamy crema.
  4. Consider adjustments: If the shot is watery, use more coffee grounds or a finer grind. If the shot takes longer than 30 seconds, reduce the amount of coffee grounds or use a coarser grind.

Tips:

  • Practice makes perfect: it may take a few tries to get the grind and tamping just right.
  • Invest in a good espresso machine for the best results, but portable espresso makers can still yield decent espresso.
  • If you're new to espresso, consider buying pre-ground espresso for easier preparation.
