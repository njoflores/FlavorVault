Onigiri Bento Lunch

Onigiri Bento is a typical Japanese lunch that includes rice balls filled with various fillings, side dishes, and nori seaweed. Perfect for a quick and easy lunch, it can be prepped the night before, leaving you with minimal work in the morning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cup steamed rice
  • 1 Umeboshi pickled sour plum
  • 2 Tbsp Katsuobushi dried bonito flakes
  • 1/2 tsp soy sauce
  • Salt
  • 1 sheet Nori roasted dried seaweed

    • Side Dishes:

  • 1/2 recipe Rolled Chicken with Vegetables
  • 1 boiled egg
  • Salt
  • Black sesame seeds
  • 3 florets broccoli
  • 1/2 tsp ground sesame seeds
  • 1/4 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp mayo
  • 3 slices cucumber
  • 3 cherry tomatoes

Instructions:

  1. Prepare for Onigiri: Remove the pit from the Umeboshi (pickled plum). In a small bowl, mix the Katsuobushi (bonito flakes) with soy sauce.
  2. Wet your hands with water and sprinkle them with salt. Place some rice in one hand and press it to form a triangle shape. Make a small well in the center of the rice ball and add the filling. Close the well, reshape the rice, and wrap it with a sheet of roasted seaweed.
  3. Prepare the side dishes: Make the Rolled Chicken with Vegetables following the recipe.
  4. Boil the egg, cut it in half, and sprinkle with salt and black sesame seeds.
  5. Blanch the broccoli, then mix it with ground sesame seeds, soy sauce, and mayo.
  6. Assemble the Bento: Arrange the Onigiri rice balls and side dishes, along with sliced cucumber and cherry tomatoes for a splash of color.

Tips:

  • Prepare your side dishes the night before for a quicker and easier morning prep.
  • For a more filling option, try using salmon flakes as a filling for the onigiri.
  • Make sure not to put leftover food from the previous day in your bento box, as it can lead to bacterial growth.
