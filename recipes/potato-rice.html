Satsumaimo Gohan (Japanese Sweet Potato Rice)

Satsumaimo Gohan is a simple yet flavorful rice dish made with sweet potatoes, a seasonal favorite in Japan during the fall and winter. The naturally sweet, sticky texture of satsumaimo pairs wonderfully with steamed rice. This dish is easy to make with a rice cooker and requires only a few ingredients. Enjoy this delightful seasonal treat as a side dish to grilled fish or meat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice (rice cooker cup = 180ml)
  • 1 Satsumaimo (Japanese sweet potato)
  • 1 tbsp sake (optional)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Black sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice and place it in the rice cooker bowl. Add water to the "line 2" inside the rice cooker bowl. Let the rice soak while preparing the rest of the ingredients.
  2. Cut the satsumaimo into 1/2-inch cubes and soak them in water for 10 minutes. Strain the cubes after soaking.
  3. Add sake (if using) and salt to the rice cooker bowl, then stir. Place the prepared satsumaimo on top of the rice.
  4. Cook the rice and sweet potatoes in the rice cooker. Once finished, serve the rice in bowls and top with black sesame seeds for garnish.

Tips:

  • Adding sake enhances the aroma of the rice, but it can be skipped if preferred.
  • If you want to make the dish more savory, try adding a little bit of soy sauce along with the salt.
  • This rice dish pairs wonderfully with grilled fish or meat and any seasonal vegetables.
